How It Started

One video. One pair of Vans. One deadline.

April 2019. Scrolling through YouTube, Aidan Copeland came across a custom sneaker video by an artist named Sierato — stencils, airbrushes, masking tape, toothbrushes turning a blank shoe into something else entirely. He'd always loved art, and with 8th grade graduation two months out, he set himself a deadline: finish a pair of his own in time to wear it.

A pair of Vans. A 12-pack of Angelus paint. Months of trial and error using the techniques he'd watched on repeat. On the morning of graduation, they were finished — not identical to Sierato's, but entirely his own.

What started as one pair for himself became one for a friend's brother, then teammates, then customers he'd never met. Cope Custom Kicks was never planned as a business — it became one, one commission at a time.
